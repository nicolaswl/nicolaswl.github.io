Ethics In Design

Making ethical design choices is not optional. Here's some links to get you started.

How Design Designs Us: Part 3 | The Ethics of Design

Ethics and design are deeply intertwined. Acaroglu makes this case emphatically by suggesting that global decisions require global conversation. Everybody should have a say in deciding how and if designs will affect us and our children. Do we even need or want to achieve technological singularity? These are tough questions that everybody has a say in. Apathy is a vote for the status quo.

How Technology is Hijacking Your Mind — from a Magician and Google Design Ethicist

Tristan Harris, most well known for his role in Netflix’s Social Dilemma, lays out the myriad of ways in which our technology “hijacks” our minds. All of our technologies offer us what we think we want whether it be the best restaurant through Yelp, our next friend on Facebook, or even our next post on an endlessly scrolling Instagram feed. However, what we want out of our technology rarely aligns with what the technology wants out of us. Harris outlines this disconnect with ten eye-opening examples.

Principles Of Humane Technology

The Center for Humane Technology is leading the way in changing tech culture and the way we think about the value of technology. Here, they challenge many misguided assumptions designers operate under when designing new technologies and suggest alternatives that reflect our rapidly evolving world and culture. The organization also offers a set of guiding principles for designers to operate under when designing technologies: a code of conduct for those who accept the responsibility for designs that are “increasingly shaping our social fabric.”
